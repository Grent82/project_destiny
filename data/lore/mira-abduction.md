# The Taking of Mira Valdris

## Canonical Source

This document restates `docs/narrative.md` (§4, "The Protagonist — House Valdris") in quest-planning form. `docs/narrative.md` is the sole narrative source of truth. If anything here ever drifts from it, fix this document, not the other way around.

## The Timeline

- **~70 years ago:** Gregor Valdris founds House Valdris in the post-Breach city.
- **18 months ago:** The Gilded Court moves against House Valdris. The proximate cause is commercial — the Valdriss were undercutting Court-aligned distributors. The real cause is structural: the Valdriss were accumulating multi-district leverage the Court could not have managed through normal social pressure within a decade.
- **The method:** a manufactured debt claim — a forged promissory note backed by enough legitimate-seeming documentation to survive initial scrutiny — presented to the Compact's financial magistrates before the family could mount a legal challenge.
- **The same night:** Edric Valdris (the father) dies. Official cause: heart failure. His body was unavailable for independent examination. Witnesses describe armed men in unmarked livery taking Mira — the eldest child, the one Edric was grooming as heir — that same night.
- **Three days later:** Cael Valdris (eldest brother) is killed in the Harbor Ward. Officially a street robbery. The circumstances do not hold up to scrutiny.
- **The protagonist** (youngest, least visible) was off-district the night Edric died — the only reason they are still standing. No one thought the youngest was worth planning around.

## Why Mira, Specifically

She was not incidental collateral of the raid. She was the piece worth keeping alive: proof the Court could produce if the family ever mounted a real legal challenge to the debt claim, and a warning to anyone who might have helped them try. A dead hostage has no leverage — this is why she is probably still alive, and why the Court has not needed to prove it either way.

## Open Threads for Quest Design

- **Cael's murder** has zero implementation anywhere in quests, dialogue, or events — a genuinely open thread. Investigating it should lead toward evidence of the forged promissory note and, eventually, toward whoever authorized Mira's capture.
- **`npc-enemy-cassia-vorne`** ("the Court interrogator who built the case that destroyed House Valdris... professional, documented, entirely legal") has no dialogue or quest hook. She is the strongest candidate for the operation's architect — the one who specifically identified Mira as the highest-value hostage.
- **`npc-enemy-commander-fell`** (Oric Fell) and the `rival-org-gilded-hand` organization are fully authored but unused. They are the strongest candidate for who physically carried out the abduction and who guards Mira now.
- Any future confrontation with Cassia Vorne cannot be a clean villain-defeat: she operated entirely within the law. Resolution must be about exposure or leverage, not combat victory alone — per her own bio, "she will do it again to the next house that grows inconveniently large."
