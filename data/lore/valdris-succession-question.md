# The Valdris Succession Question

## The Claim

House Valdris's fall was recorded as a clean legal event: debt default, contested title, Compact Registry dissolution. The public record shows Harlen Voss, the last recognized male heir, defaulted on a civic bond in the Compact Registry three months before the Breach. The title passed through standard legal channels. House Merrow absorbed the property annexure. No surviving heir contested the dissolution.

That is the public record.

The private record, if it still exists, shows something different.

## The Erasure

Valdris heirship passed through both lines — male and female — under the original house charter. The maternal line was not extinguished with Harlen Voss. It continued. The final holder of the maternal claim was not named in any succession document filed after Year 3 of the Compact Era.

She was erased.

Not lost. Erased.

The Compact Registry accepted a filing in Year 3 that amended the Valdris charter retroactively, removing the maternal succession clause. The amendment bears the signatures of: Harlen Voss (coerced, or simply paid), Lady Sorn (present, did not object), Torvald Messe's father (who prepared the legal erasure form), and a notarial witness whose name was later struck from the filing record.

The beneficiary of this amendment: House Merrow.

## The Maternity Ledger

The ledger is a physical object — a chapel registry from The Pale District, cross-referenced with civic birth records from the Compact Registry's pre-Breach archive. Mira was compiling both when she disappeared.

It contains:
- A birth entry for a child of Valdris blood through the maternal line, three generations before the Breach
- A chain of custodianship showing that lineage was tracked, maintained, and deliberately suppressed
- A final entry, in a different hand, that references a "Rill" as the current living link

The ledger proves that the maternal line was not extinguished. It was hidden.

If the ledger reaches the Civic Magistrate's office, the entire dissolution of House Valdris becomes contestable. Every property transfer, every debt settlement, every House Merrow annexure becomes legally fragile.

## What Proving It Would Mean

A successful challenge to the Valdris dissolution would:

- Restore the house's legal standing — including all annexed property
- Expose Torvald Messe's father as the architect of the erasure (Torvald inherits the liability)
- Implicate House Merrow in a fraudulent property claim (the largest single holder of former Valdris property)
- Make Cessa Rill — if she is the living link — either a claimant or a liability depending on who reaches her first
- Create a political crisis in the Compact Registry, whose legitimacy rests on the accuracy of its records

## Who Benefits from the Secret Staying Buried

- **House Merrow** — loses the most property if the challenge succeeds
- **Torvald Messe** — inherits his father's legal exposure
- **Lady Sorn** — was present, did nothing, and signed nothing; her silence becomes complicity if the record is examined
- **The Compact Registry** — the integrity of its archive is at stake; if one erasure stands exposed, others are implied
- **Harlen Voss** — now a minor figure in House Merrow's orbit; the coercion claim would require him to testify

## The Player's Choice

The ledger is findable. It is also stealable, destroyable, and tradeable.

The player can:
- Deliver it to the Magistrate (legal restoration — maximum political chaos)
- Sell it to House Merrow (the secret stays buried; Merrow owes a debt)
- Destroy it (Mira's work ends; the erasure is permanent)
- Give it to Cessa Rill (she decides what to do with her own lineage)

There is no clean outcome. The ledger is not a solution — it is a weapon. What the player does with it is the moral center of the main quest.
